Research
Progression through exploration.

We recognise research as a bespoke practice that helps to actively elevate architectural firms and supplement the diversified design services they offer.

For practising architects, research can be a difficult concept to pin down and define but what is clear is that research is an intellectual fuel for the engine of innovation and growth for many businesses, including architects’ practices.

Drawing on our links to our network of academic and wider research organisations, we use project-specific methodology processes to explore the validity of ideas in areas such as design theory, socio-spatial relationships, post-occupancy evaluation and policy so we can help ensure - through rigoures testing - that the philosophical approaches behind design projects are well grounded.

"Architects’ practices value research and consider it to be intrinsic to their work." - RIBA

OUR PROCESS

1 - DATA COLLECTION

We undertake data gathering and measuring variables of interest using a systematic framework of quantitative and qualitative methodologies to validate research questions, test hypotheses and evaluate outcomes.

2 - DATA ANALYSIS

The process of systematically applying rigorous statistical analysis techniques to describe, evaluate and illustrate data results to analyse for patterns in observations through the entire data collection phase.
